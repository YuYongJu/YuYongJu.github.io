I build AI systems that have to actually work — in the field, under load, with real consequences. The ones I take on tend to be the ones nobody else wants: too operational for research labs, too research-shaped for product teams, too physical for SaaS builders.

Currently shipping Panoptris, an AI reasoning layer for remote critical infrastructure security. Solar farms losing copper to theft. Substations triggering false alarms at 4am. Cell towers no one's watching. The work is to triage, correlate, and respond — the kind of problem where getting it 90% right doesn't help if the 10% includes a missed intrusion.

"If the system isn't trusted enough to actually take action, it's a dashboard, not security."

Around the flagship I publish what's useful: PSAI-Bench (first industry benchmark for physical security AI), DJI Cloud Simulator (open-sourced from internal CI), and Aurelius (R&D into passive knowledge graphs for agent-velocity work).

I work alone right now. I move fast because I'm honest with myself about what's broken. I write spec before code, ship to real users early, and treat customer pain as the only valid signal. If you're operating remote infrastructure, evaluating physical security AI, or building at agent velocity — I'd like to talk.
