01 — Project Overview

Radiation oncology is a rapidly evolving field, with IJROBP publishing nearly 2,000 abstracts annually. Identifying shifts in research priorities — from emerging techniques like FLASH therapy to declining paradigms — is critical for clinicians, researchers, and funding bodies, but impossible to track manually at scale.

This project applies BERTopic with domain-optimized PubMedBERT embeddings to cluster a decade of IJROBP abstracts into coherent research themes. A novel two-phase self-validation pipeline using GPT-4o-mini scores topic quality through both statistical similarity analysis (c-TF-IDF) and semantic document-topic fit assessment, with only 2.5% strong disagreements across the sampled set.

The resulting framework surfaces candidate insights for clinician review: five emerging research frontiers, five declining areas, and a clear field-wide shift toward hypofractionation, stereotactic techniques, and immunotherapy combinations. Analyses contributed to the lab's ASTRO 2026 submission cycle (acceptance pending).

03 — Methodology
Core Model

Topic Modeling

BERTopic with domain-specific PubMedBERT embeddings captures medical semantic nuance. Documents projected via UMAP, clustered by HDBSCAN density estimation.

  • Embedding: PubMedBERT (768-dim)
  • Reduction: UMAP (15 neighbors, 0.2 min_dist)
  • Clustering: HDBSCAN (min_size=14)
  • Representation: c-TF-IDF weighting
  • Outlier reduction: 18.6% → 6.9%
Quality Assurance

LLM Validation

Novel two-phase validation using GPT-4o-mini. Phase 1 assesses topic-level coherence and merge candidates. Phase 2 scores document-topic fit at the individual level.

  • Phase 1: Topic merge assessment
  • Phase 2: Document fit scoring
  • Only 2.5% strong disagreements (1/40)
  • 196 documents flagged for clinician review

05 — Impact
Research Findings

Field-Wide Shifts

  • Clear shift toward hypofractionation and stereotactic techniques
  • Immunotherapy combinations emerging as dominant research theme
  • FLASH therapy growing fastest at +285% over the decade
  • AI/ML applications accelerating rapidly in clinical RadOnc
  • Traditional 2D/3D conformal techniques in sustained decline
